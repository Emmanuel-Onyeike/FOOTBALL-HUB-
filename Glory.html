Sex: Male

Study: CT Scan – Lumbar Spine

Date of Scan: 10th December 2025, 12:00 PM

FINDINGS

CT imaging of the lumbar spine demonstrates minor soft-tissue contusion around the lower lumbar paraspinal region confirmed by DICOM imaging. Vertebral alignment is preserved. No evidence of fracture, disc herniation, canal narrowing, or significant degenerative changes. Mild reactive muscle changes correlate with localized discomfort during sitting, bending, or twisting movements.

IMPRESSION

Findings are consistent with a minor lumbar soft-tissue contusion with associated muscle strain. This is a non-serious, self-limiting condition that typically improves over a short period with appropriate care.

POSSIBLE CAUSE

The pattern of injury suggests an acute impact to the lower back region resulting in localized tissue irritation and temporary muscular inflammation. Such minor trauma commonly leads to short-term pain and stiffness.

RECOMMENDED MANAGEMENT

  • Adequate rest and avoidance of strenuous activities for several days
  • Warm compress to the affected area 1–2 times daily
  • Mild analgesics (e.g., Paracetamol or Ibuprofen) or prescribed pain-relief medications as needed
  • Gentle stretching once symptoms begin to improve
  • Maintain proper posture when sitting or lifting

With adherence to the above measures, symptoms are expected to improve significantly within 5–10 days.

DOCTOR'S REMARK

The scan findings do not indicate any serious structural damage. The condition is mild and recoverable. Follow the recommended management plan and return for evaluation if pain worsens or persists beyond the expected recovery period.

PRESCRIPTION

  • Paracetamol 500mg – Take 1 tablet every 8 hours as needed for pain
  • Ibuprofen 400mg – Take 1 tablet every 12 hours if discomfort persists
